Teaching

My teaching focuses on helping students understand the basic concepts of structural engineering and apply them in analysis, design, and practical problem-solving. In class, I usually emphasize step-by-step explanation, clear numerical procedures, and the connection between theoretical concepts and real engineering applications.

Major Courses Taught

Theoretical Courses

  • Mechanics of Solids I
  • Mechanics of Solids II
  • Analysis of Statically Determinate Structures
  • Design of Steel Structures

Sessional Courses

  • Design of Concrete Structures Sessional I
  • Design of Concrete Structures Sessional II
  • Design of Steel Structures Sessional

Teaching Approach

My teaching approach is based on developing conceptual clarity before moving to numerical analysis and design applications. I try to make students comfortable with the fundamental assumptions, equations, and procedures used in structural engineering courses. In relevant courses and academic work, I also introduce students to practical engineering tools such as ETABS, SAP2000, and AutoCAD, so that they can relate classroom learning to structural modeling, analysis, design, and drafting practice.
